My Professional Journey

Taking a Chance

My journey in eye care began in February 2014, when I'd just moved from London to Bradford with my wife and our six-month-old baby. I was looking for more than just a job to pay the bills—I wanted something that offered growth and fulfillment. One day, while walking down Darley Street in Bradford, I saw a window advertisement for optical assistants. Even though my English was limited—I'd moved to the UK from Romania in 2008—I decided to give it a shot.

A Promising Start

I got the job and, within three months, received a pay rise and a permanent position. I loved every minute of the work and was always excited about learning something new. This enthusiasm led me to approach my supervisors about enrolling in the day release Dispensing Optician (DO) course.

Academic Pursuits

In 2015, I started the DO course at Bradford College, graduating in 2018 with distinction—all while working full-time and welcoming a second child. Towards the end of the course, there was a pivotal moment that changed everything for me.

A Defining Moment

One day at work, a patient I had previously helped was upset in the waiting area. She had been diagnosed with a retinal detachment and needed to go to A&E immediately. Despite her distress, she couldn't stop praising the optometrist for her calm and informative approach. That moment made me want to provide my patients with the same assurance and play a significant role in their lives. I made a mental note that if there was ever a chance for me to become an optometrist, I would grab it with both hands.

Career Progression

I started my optometry course at Bradford University in 2021, graduated with First Class honours in August 2022, and completed my Pre-Registration period in February 2024. This journey has shaped who I am today, and I'm forever grateful for the experiences that led me here.

Looking Ahead

This experience inspired me to establish I Care Services, where I can play a pivotal role in the lives of my patients and community. I'm motivated to keep growing and learning, helping my community and inspiring future eye care professionals by bringing quality eye care directly to people's homes—making it accessible, convenient, and personally tailored to each individual's needs.
